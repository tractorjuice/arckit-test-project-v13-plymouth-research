# **Plan to Get & Review All Plymouth Menus**

## **1. Define Scope**

Keep it tight and structured.

* **Area boundary:** Plymouth city + immediate surroundings (Saltash, Plympton, Plymstock optional).
* **Venue types:** Restaurants, cafés, pubs, takeaways, food stalls.
* **Output:** A spreadsheet/database of menus + summary insights.

---

## **2. Build a Master List of Venues**

Use multiple sources to ensure full coverage.

* **Google Maps:** Search terms — “restaurant”, “café”, “pub”, “takeaway”, “food”.
* **TripAdvisor**, **Yelp**, **OpenTable**, **Deliveroo**, **Uber Eats**, **Just Eat**.
* **Local business directories:** Plymouth City Council food business register.

**Action:** Export or manually copy each venue name + address + website link into a sheet.

---

## **3. Collect Menus (5 Methods)**

Use multiple channels because many places don’t have clean online menus.

### **A. Website Scrape (Primary)**

* Visit each venue website.
* Download or screenshot their menu (PDF, JPG, HTML).

### **B. Food Delivery Platforms**

Many restaurants have menus here even if they don’t on their own website.

* Deliveroo
* Uber Eats
* Just Eat

### **C. Social Media**

Often the only place menus exist for small independents.

* Facebook business pages
* Instagram highlights

### **D. Direct Contact**

Quick template:

> “Hi, could you send me your most current food menu? PDF or photo is perfect.”

Call or email where online menus are missing.

### **E. On-Site Visits for Outliers**

Keep this minimal. Photograph menus when:

* It's a small local café.
* They only have boards or printed menus.
* Their online presence is non-existent.

---

## **4. Organise the Menus**

Create a structured folder + spreadsheet:

### **Folders**

```
Plymouth Menus/
 ├── Pubs/
 ├── Restaurants/
 ├── Cafés/
 ├── Takeaways/
 └── Food Trucks/
```

### **Spreadsheet Columns**

* Venue name
* Category
* Address
* Website
* Menu link/file
* Last updated
* Delivery platforms
* Notes (special diets, price range, cuisine type)

---

## **5. Review & Analyse**

Use a repeatable framework:

* **Cuisine type**
* **Price levels**
* **Dietary coverage:** vegan, gluten-free, allergens
* **Menu structure:** starters, mains, desserts, specials
* **Uniqueness / signature items**
* **Seasonality & freshness**
* **Overall clarity and branding**

Optionally score menus:

* Clarity (1–5)
* Variety (1–5)
* Price fairness (1–5)
* Design quality (1–5)

---

## **6. Create Outputs**

Depending on what you need:

* **Plymouth Menu Database**
* **Top 20 menus by category**
* **Trends** — e.g., most common cuisines, average pricing, vegan options.
* **Gap analysis** — cuisines missing or underserved in Plymouth.

---

## **7. Maintenance Plan**

Menus change often. Use:

* **Quarterly checks** for all venues
* **Monthly checks** for high-turnover places (street food, takeaways)
